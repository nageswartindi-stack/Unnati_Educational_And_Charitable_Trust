Our JourneyUNNATI began in 2021 in Kalaburagi during
the COVID pandemic, with a simple giveaway
event by a group of generous and like-minded
individuals. What started as a one-time act of
kindness soon grew into a continuous
commitment to uplift society.
We began by offering Future-Ready Skills
training programs for underprivileged
students, covering confidence building, study
skills and career awareness. So far, 50+
programs have benefitted over 2,500 students.
Mental Health Awareness has been one of our
key focus areas throughout this journey.
In 2024, UNNATI evolved into a registered Trust,
expanding its contributions to include Meal
distribution, Blanket drives, Educational kits
and Hygiene kits for underprivileged
communities.

Menstrual Hygiene
awareness And Kit
Distribution programOur Women’s Health and Hygiene Awareness and
Kit Distribution Program is designed to empower
women with the knowledge, confidence, and
resources they need to take charge of their
personal wellbeing. Through interactive
awareness sessions, we address important topics
such as menstrual hygiene, common health
issues, safe hygiene practices, and the
importance of self-care. We work to break social
taboos and create a comfortable, supportive
space where women can openly discuss their
concerns. To strengthen this initiative, we provide
hygiene kits containing essential items that help
women maintain dignity, comfort, and daily
health. By combining education with practical
support, our program aims to improve the overall
quality of life for women in underserved
communities and promote long-term, positive
health habits.
